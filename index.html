About Me

Hello! i’m Virat Kohali.

Virat Kohli was born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India. He showed an early aptitude for cricket and began his professional career at a young age.
Kohli made his debut for the Indian national cricket team in August 2008 in an ODI against Sri Lanka. He made his Test debut in August 2011 against the West Indies.
Virat Kohli is also known for his charitable efforts. He and his wife, Anushka Sharma, have been involved in various philanthropic activities.
Kohli is known for his exceptional batting skills and has numerous records to his name. He has scored over 77 international centuries (both ODIs and Tests combined) and is considered one of the best batsmen in modern cricket.

Sachin Tendulkar

During the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, Sachin Tendulkar, one of the greatest cricketers of all time, scored a total of 482 runs in 9 matches. Tendulkar's performances were exceptional, and he was a key contributor to India's successful campaign in the tournament. His consistent run-scoring played a crucial role in India winning the 2011 Cricket World Cup, and he received widespread acclaim for his performances during the tournament.

Gautam Gambhir

During the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, Gautam Gambhir was one of the standout performers for the Indian cricket team. He scored a total of 393 runs in 9 matches during the tournament. Gambhir's consistent and impactful batting played a crucial role in India's successful campaign, and he was particularly instrumental in the final match against Sri Lanka, where he scored 97 runs. His performance in the final was vital in helping India secure their second Cricket World Cup title in 2011.

Virender Sehwag

During the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, Virender Sehwag, one of India's explosive opening batsmen, scored a total of 380 runs in 9 matches. Sehwag's aggressive style of batting and ability to provide explosive starts at the top of the order were significant for India during the tournament. His contributions played a crucial role in India's successful campaign, which culminated in them winning the 2011 Cricket World Cup.

Yuvraj Singh

During the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, Yuvraj Singh played a pivotal role for the Indian cricket team. He scored a total of 362 runs in 9 matches during the tournament. Yuvraj's performances with both the bat and the ball were crucial in India's successful campaign, and he was named the Player of the Tournament for his all-round contributions. His batting and crucial wickets played a significant role in India's journey to winning the 2011 Cricket World Cup.

Virat Kohli

During the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, Virat Kohli, who was a relatively young and emerging talent at that time, scored a total of 282 runs in 9 matches. Kohli's performances were promising, and he played a supporting role in India's successful campaign, contributing with some valuable runs in crucial matches. India won the tournament, and it was one of the significant moments in Kohli's early international career.

MS Dhoni

During the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, MS Dhoni, the captain of the Indian cricket team, scored a total of 273 runs in 9 matches. Dhoni's contributions with the bat were significant during the tournament, including crucial innings in the knockout stages and the final match against Sri Lanka, where he scored an unbeaten 91 runs to guide India to victory.
